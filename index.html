"I will do anything in my power to protect children who are discarded by their parents for being LGBT"

- Bea Arthur (third from left)

Timeline of Bea Arthur

  • 1922 - Born in Brooklyn, New York.
  • 1934 - She was the tallest girl in her school with a height of 5'9.
  • 1964 - Became truly famous as Yente, the Matchmaker in "Fiddler on the Roof".
  • 1966 - Won a Tony Award for the role of Vera Charles in the Broadway Musical "Mame".
  • 1971 - Appeared on the hit sitcom All in the Family (1971) as Maude Findlay.
  • 1972 - The guest appearance led to her own series "Maude" that ran for 6 years.
  • 1974 - Repeated the role of Vera Charles in the film version of "Mame".
  • 1985 - "The Golden Girls" made its debut and became an immediate hit, running for 7 years.
  • 2001 - Received a Tony Award Nomination for a one-woman stage show.
  • 2003 - The three Golden Girls reunited for their reunion on the Lifetime Channel.
  • 2009 - At age 86, Arthur died of cancer at home with her family. In her will, she left $300,000 to New York's Ali Forney Center, an organization supporting homeless LGBT youths.
